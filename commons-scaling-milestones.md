# Commons Scaling Milestones

**Version 1.0**  
*Created: 2026-03-11*

---

## Purpose

"What does success look like?" The answer changes depending on how big the Commons is. This document defines what a thriving Commons looks like at each growth stage, with appropriate metrics, expectations, and structural adaptations.

---

## Growth Stages Overview

| Stage | Members | Characteristics |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Seed | 1-10 | Intimate, handcrafted culture |
| Sprout | 11-50 | Growing community, forming identity |
| Sapling | 51-200 | Established, structured, diverse |
| Tree | 201-500 | Stable, multi-faceted |
| Forest | 500+ | Self-sustaining ecosystem |

---

## Stage 1: Seed (1-10 Members)

### What It Looks Like

- Everyone knows everyone
- High intimacy, low overhead
- Informal governance works
- Direct relationships matter most

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Active members | 80%+ | Intimacy depends on it |
| Posts/week | 20+ | Active dialogue |
| Tier progression | N/A (informal) | Let roles emerge naturally |
| Ritual attendance | 70%+ | Rituals build culture |
| Governance | Lazy consensus | Too small for formal voting |

### Structural Notes

- No formal Council yet
- Keep channels minimal (2-4 max)
- Focus: Relationship building
- Decision: Anyone can propose, anyone can object

---

## Stage 2: Sprout (11-50 Members)

### What It Looks Like

- Critical mass reached
- Subgroups forming naturally
- Need for explicit norms
- First governance roles emerge

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Active members | 60%+ | Growing but not guaranteed |
| Posts/week | 70-140 | Sustained conversation |
| Tier progression | 15%+/month | Healthy upward movement |
| Ritual attendance | 50%+ | Community cohesion |
| Voting participation | 60%+ | Governance matters |
| Moderation incidents | <3/month | Culture still forming |

### Structural Notes

- **Council forms** (3 members)
- First formal roles: Moderators
- Channel expansion (5-8 channels)
- Governance: Simple majority votes
- Focus: Onboarding + culture codification

---

## Stage 3: Sapling (51-200 Members)

### What It Looks Like

- Multiple distinct subcommunities
- Governance infrastructure needed
- Tier system fully operational
- Need for delegated roles

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Active members | 50%+ | Harder to maintain at scale |
| Posts/week | 140-350 | Diverse conversations |
| Tier progression | 10%+/month | Continued growth |
| Ritual attendance | 40%+ | Harder to gather everyone |
| Voting participation | 50%+ | Many voters needed |
| Moderation incidents | <5%/month | Manageable volume |

### Structural Notes

- **Council expands** (5 members)
- Working Groups form
- Channel tier system operational
- First specialized roles: Welcomers, Mentors
- Focus: Balance inclusion + efficiency

---

## Stage 4: Tree (201-500 Members)

### What It Looks Like

- Self-sustaining ecosystem
- Multiple power centers
- Formal processes required
- Need for representation

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Active members | 40%+ | Scale challenge real |
| Posts/week | 350-700 | High activity |
| Tier progression | 8%+/month | Slow but steady |
| Ritual attendance | 30%+ | Multiple ritual times needed |
| Voting participation | 40%+ | Representative democracy |
| Moderation incidents | <5%/month | Professional mod team |

### Structural Notes

- **Council: 7 members** (max)
- Moderation team (not just Council)
- Automated onboarding flows
- Channel hierarchy formalized
- Focus: Sustainability + representation

---

## Stage 5: Forest (500+ Members)

### What It Looks Like

- Self-governing at scale
- Culture embedded in systems
- Multiple Commons within the Commons
- Network effects active

### Success Criteria

| Metric | Target | Rationale |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Active members | 35%+ | Natural decay at scale |
| Posts/week | 700+ | Exponential channels |
| Tier progression | 5%+/month | Slower but meaningful |
| Ritual attendance | 25%+ | Multiple simultaneous events |
| Voting participation | 30%+ | Quorum-based |
| Moderation incidents | <5%/month | Professional ops |

### Structural Notes

- Federated governance (sub-Councils)
- Paid coordination roles possible
- Sophisticated automation
- Multiple simultaneous spaces
- Focus: Meta-governance + interoperability

## Transition Checklist

### Seed → Sprout

- [ ] First Council elected
- [ ] Moderation system formalized
- [ ] Tier system operational
- [ ] 3+ rituals established
- [ ] Welcome process documented

### Sprout → Sapling

- [ ] Council expanded to 5
- [ ] Working Groups possible
- [ ] 8+ channels active
- [ ] Governance voting regular
- [ ] Onboarding flow documented

### Sapling → Tree

- [ ] Council at max (7)
- [ ] Dedicated moderation team
- [ ] Automated member tracking
- [ ] Quarterly reviews standard
- [ ] Multiple ritual times

### Tree → Forest

- [ ] Sub-council structure
- [ ] Professional operations
- [ ] Cross-Commons connections
- [ ] Network effects visible
- [ ] Legacy systems in place

## Warning Signs at Each Stage

### Seed Stage

- Single member dominates
- No new members in 30 days
- All conversation in DMs

### Sprout Stage

- Churn exceeds growth
- Governance decisions stall
- Subgroups isolated from each other

### Sapling Stage

- Tier progression stalls
- Council disconnected from members
- Multiple echo chambers

### Tree Stage

- Governance paralysis
- Moderation backlog
- Quality decline in channels

### Forest Stage

- Fragmentation
- Loss of Commons identity
- Member exploitation

## Adaptation Guidelines

### When to Adjust Targets

- **1.5x rule:** If a metric is 50% above/below target for 2 months, recalibrate
- **Growth pause:** Accept lower metrics during rapid growth phases
- **External shocks:** Adjust for platform changes, holidays, etc.

### When to Restructure

- Governance failing = add capacity
- Moderation overwhelmed = add people
- Channels chaotic = consolidate
- Participation falling = simplify

---

## Long-Term Vision

At each stage, ask:

1. **Are we still living our values?**
2. **Is the Commons serving members?**
3. **Are new leaders emerging?**
4. **Is culture strengthening or weakening?**
5. **Would we join this Commons today?**

Numbers are indicators, not the goal. A Commons of 10 deeply connected members is as valid as one of 500.
