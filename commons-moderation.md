# Commons Moderation

**Version 1.0**  
*Effective: 2026-03-10*

---

## Overview

This document details the moderation system referenced in Article III of the Charter. Moderation in The Commons is community-led, light-touch, and restorative.

---

## Principles

1. **Community-Led** — Moderators are first among equals, not rulers
2. **Light Touch** — Defaults to allowing speech, intervenes only on clear violations
3. **Restorative First** — Focus on fixing harm, not punishment
4. **Transparent** — All moderation decisions are logged and appealable
5. **Graduated Response** — Escalates from warning to removal

---

## The Moderator Code

All Moderators commit to:

- Serve the community, not personal agenda
- Apply rules consistently and fairly
- Recuse from cases involving personal conflicts
- Accept appeals gracefully
- Step down when burned out
- Maintain confidentiality
- Document all actions
- Stay calm under pressure

## Moderation Tiers

### Level 1: Private Note

**Trigger:**
- First-time minor norm violation
- Unintentional disruption
- Unfamiliarity with channel norms
- Accidental offense

**Response:**
- Private message to member
- Explain what happened
- Provide guidance on correct behavior
- No public record

**Example:**
> "Hey [Name], noticed your message in The Forge may have missed the channel norms. We're more focused on technical depth here. No worries—just wanted to point you toward The Hearth for casual chat. Let me know if you have questions!"

---

### Level 2: Public Reminder + Brief Mute

**Trigger:**
- Second minor violation
- Ignoring Level 1 warning
- Mild disruption to channel
- Repeated off-topic posts

**Response:**
- Public reminder (brief, private)
- 24-hour mute from affected channel
- Logged in moderation record

**Example:**
> "[Name], this is your second reminder about [norm]. I've temporarily muted you from [channel] for 24 hours to give space. Feel free to return after. Questions? Message me."

---

### Level 3: Temporary Suspension

**Trigger:**
- First-time serious violation (harassment, deception)
- Repeated Level 2 violations
- Intentional harm to member(s)
- Violation of Tier 3 confidentiality

**Response:**
- 72-hour suspension from all Commons spaces
- Direct message explaining situation
- Logged with full context
- Review scheduled for return

**Example:**
> "[Name], your recent actions in The Square constitute [violation]. This is a serious matter. You've been suspended from The Commons for 72 hours. After this period, we'll discuss what needs to happen for you to return. Contact [Council contact] if you wish to appeal."

---

### Level 4: Permanent Exclusion

**Trigger:**
- Repeated Level 3 violations
- Severe harm to member(s)
- System gaming or manipulation
- Attempting to evade previous bans
- Core Charter violation

**Response:**
- Permanent removal from all spaces
- All access revoked
- Logged permanently
- Appeal possible after 90 days

**Example:**
> "[Name], after multiple violations and review, you've been permanently excluded from The Commons. This decision is logged and may be appealed after 90 days. We wish you well elsewhere."

## Specific Violation Responses

### Commerce (Selling/Advertising)

| Occurrence | Response |
|------------|----------|
| 1st | Level 1: Private note + remove content |
| 2nd | Level 2: Reminder + 24h mute |
| 3rd+ | Level 3: 72h suspension |

*Exception: Approved collaborative projects in Working Groups*

---

### Harassment

| Severity | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Mild (unintended) | Level 1: Private note |
| Moderate (repeated) | Level 2-3: Mute + suspension |
| Severe (threats) | Level 4: Immediate permanent exclusion |

---

### Impersonation

| Type | Response |
|------|----------|
| Accidental similar name | Level 1: Private note to change |
| Intentional impersonation | Level 3-4: Suspension or exclusion |

---

### Spam

| Volume | Response |
|--------|----------|
| 1-3 messages | Level 1: Remove + note |
| 4-10 messages | Level 2: 24h mute |
| 10+ messages | Level 3: 72h suspension |

## Emergency Moderation

### Definition
- Active harm in progress
- Multiple simultaneous violations
- Threat to Commons safety
- Technical security breach

### Emergency Powers

| Situation | Authority | Time Limit |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Spam flood | Any Mod | Immediate, then 24h report |
| Serious violation | Mod team | 72h max, then review |
| Governance attack | Council | 7 days, then Assembly |

### Emergency Process
1. Mod team acts immediately to stop harm
2. Post explanation within 24h
3. Full review within 7 days
4. Can be overridden by 2/3 Assembly vote

## Appeals Process

### How to Appeal

1. **Within 14 days** of decision
2. **Message Council** or post in Plaza (if public case)
3. **Include:** What happened, why you disagree, what you'd want

### Appeal Review

1. **3 Council members** review (not involved in original)
2. **7-day consideration** period
3. **Simple majority** decides
4. **Decision is final** (unless new evidence)

### Appeal Outcomes

- **Upheld** — Original decision stands
- **Modified** — Reduced penalty
- **Reversed** — Full restoration
- **New evidence** — Re-review with new info

## Moderation Logs

### What Gets Logged

- All Level 2+ actions
- Member name/ID
- Date and time
- Channel/space affected
- Violation type
- Action taken
- Moderator name
- Appeal status

### Privacy

- Logs are internal only
- Not shared with general membership
- Available to Council on request
- Retained for 2 years

---

## Moderator Selection

### Requirements

- Contributor tier or above
- 60+ days in Commons
- No active violations
- Community trust

### Process

1. **Nomination:** Self or 2+ members
2. **Review:** Council evaluates
3. **Training:** Review Moderator Code + procedures
4. **Probation:** 30-day initial term
5. **Confirmation:** Full status after probation

### Removal

- Resignation (with notice)
- Recall by 2/3 Council vote
- Burnout/ inactivity (auto after 30 days absent)

---

## Conflict of Interest

Moderators must recuse from cases involving:

- Personal relationships with involved parties
- Direct conflicts in the matter
- Previous personal disputes

If all Moderators are conflicted, Council appoints ad-hoc reviewer.

---

## Transparency Reports

Quarterly, the Council publishes:

- Total moderation actions by level
- Most common violation types
- Appeals and outcomes
- Trends and concerns

This is shared in The Plaza.
